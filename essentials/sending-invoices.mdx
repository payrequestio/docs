## Email Delivery

### Sending via PayRequest
The most common way to deliver invoices:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Invoice">
    Double-check all details including customer email, amounts, and due dates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize Email">
    Add a personal message or use one of our professional email templates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send Invoice">
    Click **Send Invoice** to deliver immediately, or schedule for later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track Delivery">
    Monitor delivery status and see when customers view your invoice.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Email Templates
Choose from professional templates or create your own:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Standard Templates">

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Custom Templates">

### Email Customization
Personalize each invoice email:

```
Subject: Invoice #INV-2024-001 from [Your Company Name]

Hi [Customer Name],

Please find attached your invoice for [Service/Product Description].

Payment is due by [Due Date]. You can pay securely online using the link below:

[Pay Now Button]

If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to contact me.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Company Name]
```

## Best Practices

### Timing Your Invoice Delivery
<Tip>
  Send invoices immediately after completing work or delivering products to maintain momentum and improve cash flow.
</Tip>

- **Service-based businesses**: Send within 24 hours of completion
- **Product sales**: Send immediately upon shipment
- **Recurring billing**: Send 3-5 days before due date
- **Project milestones**: Send upon milestone completion

### Professional Communication
- Use clear, professional subject lines
- Include all necessary payment information
- Maintain a friendly but professional tone
- Always include your contact information
- Thank customers for their business

### Avoiding Common Mistakes
<Warning>
  Double-check customer email addresses and invoice amounts before sending to avoid delays and confusion.
</Warning>

- Verify customer contact information
- Check invoice calculations
- Include clear payment instructions
- Set realistic due dates
- Follow up consistently but not aggressively
